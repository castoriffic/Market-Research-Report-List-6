<p><p>The Real-Time PCR reagents and kits market is primarily segmented into two types: One-step Real-Time PCR and Two-step Real-Time PCR. One-step Real-Time PCR combines all necessary reagents into a single reaction, allowing for quicker and more efficient amplification and detection of nucleic acids. In contrast, Two-step Real-Time PCR involves separate stages for reverse transcription and PCR amplification, providing greater flexibility and specificity. These methods cater to diverse applications in research, clinical diagnostics, and genetic analysis.</p></p>
