<p><p>The roughness meters market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact roughness meters utilize a stylus that physically touches the surface to measure roughness by tracing its profile. In contrast, non-contact roughness meters employ optical or laser technologies to assess surface texture without physical contact. Both types serve various industries, such as manufacturing and quality control, offering critical insights into surface characteristics that influence product performance and aesthetics.</p></p>
