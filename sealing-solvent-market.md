<p><p>The sealing solvent market can be categorized into three main types based on curing properties. Uncured types remain pliable and are ideal for applications requiring flexibility; they do not harden after application. Fast curing types quickly solidify, offering rapid adhesion and efficient project timelines, making them suitable for urgent repairs. Slow curing types take longer to set, providing more working time and often superior bonds, which is advantageous for intricate applications where precision is critical. Each type serves distinct industry needs.</p></p>
