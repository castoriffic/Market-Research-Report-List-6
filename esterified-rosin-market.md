<p><p>The esterified rosin market is segmented into two main types based on softening points: low softening point and high softening point. Low softening point esters are typically used in adhesives, inks, and paints, offering better flexibility and lower viscosity for easier application. In contrast, high softening point esters provide enhanced heat resistance and strength, making them suitable for applications requiring durability, such as in coatings and industrial adhesives. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, emphasizing functionality and performance.</p></p>
