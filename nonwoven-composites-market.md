<p><p>The nonwoven composites market is categorized into several types: meltblown, spunbonded, staples, and others. Meltblown fabrics are created by extruding molten polymer through fine nozzles, resulting in highly efficient filtration materials. Spunbonded fabrics involve the bonding of continuous filaments, offering strength and durability for various applications. Staple nonwovens are made from short fibers, providing versatility for uses like wipes and textiles. Other categories may include unique techniques such as needle-punched or hydroentangled fabrics, catering to specialized needs in various industries.</p></p>
