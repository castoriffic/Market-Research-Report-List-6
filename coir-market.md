<p><p>The coir market is primarily divided into two types: brown fiber and white fiber. Brown fiber, sourced from mature coconuts, is coarse and strong, making it ideal for products like doormats, brushes, and erosion control. In contrast, white fiber, derived from young coconuts, is softer and finer, often used in crafts, upholstery, and horticultural applications. Both types play significant roles in various industries, with brown fiber generally commanding a higher price due to its durability and versatility.</p></p>
